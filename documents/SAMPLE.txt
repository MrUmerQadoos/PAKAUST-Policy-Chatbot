PAF-IAST ACADEMIC RULES AND REGULATIONS FOR BACHELOR PROGRAMS
Version 1.1 - Approved by 6th Academic Council Meeting on 30th November 2021

=== SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION ===

Academic Year:
The academic year comprises mainly two regular semesters: Fall and Spring.
- Fall Semester: 18 weeks (September/October start)
- Spring Semester: 18 weeks (February/March start)
- Summer Semester: 9 weeks (optional)

Credit Hour System:
- One credit hour = one hour of theory teaching per week throughout the semester
- One classroom hour = 60 minutes including 10-minute break
- One credit hour of lab work = 3 contact hours of laboratory work per week

Medium of Instruction:
- All courses except language courses and Islamic Studies are taught in English
- Islamic Studies can be taught in student's choice of language

Class Scheduling:
- All classes held at scheduled hours as per timetable
- Rescheduling requires approval from Head of Department/Chairman
- Classes can be scheduled for 90 minutes (1.5 periods) with approval
- Weekend/holiday classes possible if regular weeks insufficient

Course Load:
- Regular semester load determined by Scheme of Studies
- Students can register 12-18 credit hours normally
- Up to 21 credit hours allowed for CGPA ≥ 3.50
- Must complete prerequisites before registering for a course

Attendance Requirements:
- Mandatory attendance: 75%
- Students aim for 100% attendance in lectures, labs, tutorials, seminars
- Below 75% attendance = ineligible for final examination = F grade
- Below 65% attendance = automatic F grade

Bachelor's Degree Requirements:
- Minimum duration: 4 years (8 regular semesters)
- Maximum duration: 6 academic years
- Minimum credit hours: 130
- Minimum CGPA required: 2.00
- Must complete 500 hours of internship
- Must clear all courses in Scheme of Studies
- Good academic and disciplinary standing required

Clearance Requirements:
- Students withdrawing or completing must obtain clearance from all departments
- Security deposit forfeited if clearance not completed

=== SECTION 2: GRADING AND GPA CALCULATIONS ===

Grading System:
PAF-IAST follows Relative Grading System

Grade Scale:
- A: 4.00 points (85-100%)
- A-: 3.67 points (80-84%)
- B+: 3.33 points (75-79%)
- B: 3.00 points (70-74%)
- B-: 2.67 points (65-69%)
- C+: 2.33 points (60-64%)
- C: 2.00 points (55-59%)
- D: 1.00 points (50-54%)
- F: 0.00 points (Below 50%)

Special Grades (no grade points):
- W: Withdrawal from course
- Q: Completion of non-credit course
- I: Incomplete (medical/family emergency)
- IP: In Progress (for FYP-I)
- T: Transfer credit

GPA Calculation:
- SGPA (Semester GPA) = Sum of (Grade Points × Credit Hours) / Total Credits in Semester
- CGPA (Cumulative GPA) = Weighted average of all semesters
- Course repetition: Both grades visible on transcript, best grade used in CGPA calculation

=== SECTION 3: TRANSFER POLICY ===

Credit Transfer from Another University:
- Student must have studied at Fachhochschule model institute
- Seat must be available at PAF-IAST
- Maximum 40% of total credit hours can be transferred
- Minimum grade B (3.00 GPA) required for transfer
- Only credits transfer, not CGPA
- NOC required from previous institute (no disciplinary action, no dues)
- Maximum degree duration counted from first admission date

Visiting Student Credits:
- Students can take courses at other institutions with prior approval
- Minimum grade B required
- Maximum 40% credit transfer limit applies
- Detailed course outlines required for evaluation
- Dean approval required

=== SECTION 4: REGISTRATION ===

First Semester Registration:
- Must register for all courses in Scheme of Studies
- Cannot drop courses in first semester

Registration Procedure:
1. Clear all previous fees and dues
2. Register courses online
3. Pay semester fees by due date

Registration Timeline:
- Provisional registration: Starts 2 weeks before semester
- Final registration: Until 3rd day of semester
- Add/Drop period: First 2 weeks of semester
- Fee payment: By due date on voucher

Course Load Rules:
- Regular students: All courses per Scheme of Studies
- Can add repeat courses if total ≤ 21 credit hours
- Academic deficiency students: Consult with advisor
- Can improve courses with C or below grade (max 12 credit hours)

Theory and Lab Registration:
- Must register for both theory and lab components together
- Cannot withdraw from only one component
- Can register if passed theory part of prerequisite
- Grade improvement requires re-registering both components

Final Year Project (FYP) Registration:
- Offered in second-last regular semester
- Spans two semesters (FYP-I and FYP-II)
- Requirements for FYP-I: CGPA ≥ 2.00 and 90 credit hours completed
- FYP-II requires successful completion of FYP-I

Adding or Dropping Courses:
- Allowed within first 2 weeks of regular semester
- Requires advisor recommendation and HoD/Chairman approval
- First semester students cannot drop coursess
- Summer semester: No dropping allowed

Course Withdrawal:
- Withdrawal allowed from week 2 to end of week 10
- Results in W grade (appears on transcript)
- May delay graduation
- Fee non-refundable
- First-year students cannot withdraw in first semester
- Financial aid recipients need Dean approval for withdrawal

Course Repeat and Replacement:
- Can repeat courses with C or below for improvement
- F or W grade in core course: Must repeat same course
- Core courses cannot be replaced
- Discontinued courses can be replaced with similar course (HoD approval)
- Best grade used in CGPA calculation
- Both attempts visible on transcript
- Can repeat after graduation within 6-year limit

Semester Freeze:
- Means unregistering all courses after add/drop period
- Tuition fee not refunded
- Allowed for attendance below 75% on medical/unavoidable grounds
- Requires Rector approval

Admission Closure:
- Results in termination from program
- Automatic closure after maximum duration (6 years)
- Can be closed by Disciplinary Committee
- Student can request closure for personal reasons
- New admission closed if eligibility criteria not met

=== SECTION 5: ACADEMIC ASSESSMENT ===

Assessment Components:

Theory Courses:
- Final Exam: 40-50%
- Mid-term Exams (max 2): 20-30%
- Sessional (quizzes, assignments, projects): 20-30%
- Pre-final total: 50-60%

Lab Courses:
- Final Exam: 40-50%
- Mid-term Exams (max 2): 20-30%
- Sessional (minimum 12 lab tasks): 20-30%
- Pre-final total: 50-60%

Grading:
- Relative grading system used
- Letter grade awarded based on overall performance
- All assessment results shared except final exam
- Final exam viewable through rechecking process

Incomplete (I) Grade Policy:
- Awarded for missing final exam due to medical reasons or death of first-degree relative
- Other emergencies require Board of Faculty approval
- Makeup exam within 6 weeks of next semester
- Failure to take makeup = F grade
- Pre-final marks cannot be altered
- I grade replaced with earned grade after makeup

Academic Warning:
- Issued when SGPA falls below 2.00 in any semester
- Issued when CGPA falls below 2.00
- Issued when F grade earned in any course
- Warning count resets to zero if CGPA reaches 2.00

Academic Probation:
- SGPA below 2.00 for two consecutive regular semesters
- CGPA falls below 2.00 at end of any regular semester
- Can be placed on probation without prior warning

Academic Withdrawal:
- CGPA below 1.00 in first semester
- SGPA below 2.00 for first two consecutive regular semesters
- CGPA below 2.00 for three consecutive regular semesters after first semester

Result Declaration:
- Controller of Examination publishes results
- Grades awarded by instructor, discussed in department meeting
- HoD can suggest changes but cannot alter grades
- Final decision with instructor
- Dean approves grades, deficiency cases, probation, withdrawal

Retotaling Process:
- Application within 7 days of result announcement
- Retotaling fee required
- Committee: Controller of Exam (Convener), HoD, Subject Specialist
- Committee recounts marks, identifies unmarked portions
- Cannot discuss outcome with student
- Recommendations to Dean for approval
- Fee refunded if marks change

=== SECTION 6: SUMMER SEMESTER RULES ===

- 9-week optional semester
- Contact hours doubled per week
- Cannot register for new courses except:
  * Elective by distinguished scholar available only in summer
  * Need 1-2 electives to graduate
- Can repeat maximum 2 courses (with labs)
- Withdrawal allowed before end of week 5
- Cannot drop courses
- No fee refund or carry forward
- No financial assistance/scholarships
- Minimum 5 students required for course to run
- Previous semester grading scheme applied

=== SECTION 7: AWARDS AND HONORS ===

Gold Medal:
- Awarded to first position holder in each degree program
Eligibility:
- Passed all prescribed courses
- No failures, repeats, or withdrawals
- SGPA ≥ 3.00 in all semesters
- Position determined by overall GPA
- Ties broken by highest marks
- No disciplinary disqualification

Best Project Award:
- Grade A required in Final Year Project

Rector's List of Honors:
- SGPA = 4.00
- Regular course load completed
- Recognition certificate issued
- Listed on Institute website
- Published after each semester

Dean's List of Honors:
- SGPA 3.50 to 4.00
- Regular course load completed
- Recognition certificate issued
- Listed on Institute website
- Published after each semester

=== SECTION 8: ACADEMIC INTEGRITY ===

Cheating Includes:
- Giving or seeking assistance in examinations
- Unauthorized communication of answers/information
- Copying during exams, quizzes, labs
- Identity fraud for academic purposes
- Using unauthorized devices during exams
- Providing false information to teachers

Plagiarism Includes:
- Presenting others' work as your own
- Not using proper citations
- Submitting work completed by others
- Buying/selling academic materials

Other Academic Violations:
- Falsifying attendance records, grades, documents
- Fabricating data
- Bribery or paid services for assignments/tests
- Sabotaging others' work
- Removing library pages, disrupting experiments

Penalties:
Teacher can award zero in specific assessment (with HoD intimation)

Disciplinary Committee can award:
- F grade in a course
- F grade in all courses
- Fine
- Expulsion from university
- Disqualification from honors/medals/scholarships
- Combination of above

Disciplinary Committee Composition:
- Dean (Convenor) - nominated by Rector
- Controller of Examination (Secretary)
- Three Senior Faculty Members (nominated by Rector)

=== SECTION 9: ACADEMIC ADVISORS ===

Student Advisor:
- Faculty member assigned 5-10 students
- Guides students on academic and administrative issues

Advisor Duties:
- Help develop realistic educational/career plans
- Create semester-by-semester schedule plans till graduation
- Meet freshmen weekly in first semester for adjustment support
- Meet continuing students fortnightly for semester planning
- Assist in course selection based on abilities/interests
- Monitor progress toward educational/career goals
- Review grades and performance indicators each semester
- Explain and interpret Academic Rules and Regulations
- Track unsatisfactory work reports (attendance, failing grades, incompletes)
- Pay special attention to warning/probation students
- Approve educational transactions (registration, drops/adds, withdrawals)
- Maintain Advising Portfolio with performance records
- Refer students to institutional resources when needed
- Consult with faculty colleagues regularly
- Initiate I-grade cases when needed
- Resolve administrative difficulties
- Submit fortnightly signed reports to HoD/Chairman
- Submit semester compiled reports to Dean

=== SECTION 10: INTERNSHIP ===

Total Requirement:
- 18 weeks (500 hours) mandatory
- 8 weeks summer semester (after 6th semester)
- 10 weeks total summer breaks (5 weeks after 2nd semester + 5 weeks after 4th semester)

Allowed Forms:

1. Industrial Training:
- Real-time work experience in industry/R&D organization
- Hands-on observation and job execution
- National or international/multinational companies

2. Project-Based Internship:
- Applying knowledge on specific issue/problem
- Assessed by academic advisor and/or industry expert
- Developing product parts, strategies, or analyses

3. Project with Industry/Research Institute:
- Application of knowledge and skills
- Quantitative conclusions or qualitative understanding

4. Virtual Internship:
- ICT-supported environment
- Computer-aided design and simulations
- Specific project/task completion required

Internship Outcomes:
- Apply theories and skills to practical experience
- Develop new knowledge and skills
- Interact with professionals

Eligibility:
- All students (freshmen, sophomore, junior, senior)
- May require specific coursework, skillset, CGPA

Key Rules:
- Industrial training and project-based preferred
- Project/virtual internship only for one summer break
- No remuneration, transportation, or living expenses from PAF-IAST
- Individual or small team (≤4 members) preferred
- Location: Home institute, national, or international company

Student Responsibilities:
- Submit placement pro forma in first week
- Meet supervisor every two weeks
- Submit mid-term progress report
- Submit final report with detailed work and achievements

Supervisor Responsibilities:
- Provide prompt feedback
- Conduct online meeting every two weeks
- Visit company at least once during internship

Company Responsibilities:
- Provide expert supervision
- Provide necessary resources and facilities
- Ensure safety rules compliance
- Contact academic advisor if problems arise
- Send written evaluation at end

Evaluation:
- Submit internship report to employer and advisor
- Deliver presentation to advisor
- Submit evaluation form from employer
- Unsatisfactory evaluation = repeat internship
- Can request completion certificate from employer

=== SECTION 11: CODE OF CONDUCT ===

General Principles:
- Equal opportunity workplace
- No discrimination based on race, color, religion, national origin, gender
- Opportunities based on qualifications and merit

Prohibited Activities:
- Political, anti-state, unlawful, sectarian activities
- Cheating, plagiarism, harassment, cyber bullying
- Drugs, lying, stealing
- Conduct damaging Institute reputation
- Disrupting academic programs/activities

Expected Behavior:
- Show tolerance, understanding, respect for others' rights
- Resolve misunderstandings through decent discussion
- No threatening attitude/behavior
- Gender interaction within social/cultural norms
- No undue intimacy or unacceptable proximity
- Keep campus neat and tidy
- Use designated waste bins
- No damage to fixtures, furniture, equipment
- No smoking on premises
- Mobile phones off in classrooms, exam halls, labs, library
- Adhere to code of conduct in sports and extracurricular activities
- Dress decently and appropriately
- Reflect high cultural and social values

Security:
- No firearms, fireworks, dangerous devices, chemicals, explosives
- Zero-tolerance for weapons (knives, daggers)
- Always carry National ID and PAF-IAST ID cards
- No lending ID cards to others

Ragging and Bullying:
- Strictly prohibited
- Report incidents to Chairman/HoD

Media Policy:
- No posting/debating on behalf of Institute without approval
- Personal opinions must be clearly stated as such
- Be respectable and courteous

Discipline Committee:
- Handles all indiscipline matters
- Recommends appropriate corrective action
- Authorized to deal with code of conduct breaches
